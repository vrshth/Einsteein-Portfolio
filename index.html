Albert Einstein (1879–1955) was a German-born theoretical physicist who is widely regarded as one of the most influential scientists of the 20th century. His groundbreaking contributions to physics revolutionized our understanding of the universe and laid the foundation for modern physics. Born in Ulm, in the Kingdom of Württemberg in the German Empire, Einstein showed an early interest in mathematics and science. Einstein's most famous work is his theory of relativity, which he developed in the early 20th century. In 1905, he published his paper on the special theory of relativity, introducing the famous equation which expresses the equivalence of mass and energy.
